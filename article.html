Canada Has the Highest Abduction Odds of Any Country in This Study

We ranked 13 Canadian cities using the Canadian UFO Survey, which records reported sightings each year across the country.

The survey logged 4,311 sightings between 2020 and 2024. It does not flag individual reports for abduction, so total sightings per person is the closest available measure.

Every one of the 13 ranked cities would place inside the UK top three. The gap between Sherbrooke (297 per million) and Winnipeg (57 per million) is wider than the entire US ranking from first to last.

Key Findings

  • Sherbrooke leads the entire study across all three countries. 297 sightings per million residents. No US state and no UK city comes close.
  • Every Canadian city in this ranking would place inside the UK top three. The gap between Canada and the UK is not close.
  • Toronto has the most raw sightings of any Canadian city: 275. It still ranks 9th. Three million residents dilute a commanding lead into a mid-table rate.
  • Ontario produces four of the top ten cities: London (3rd), Hamilton (5th), Toronto (9th), and Ottawa (12th). Same province, same five years, wildly different rates.
  • Your lifetime chance of filing a sighting report in Sherbrooke is 0.48%. In Winnipeg, the lowest-ranked city, it is 0.09%.

Vermont Leads the US. Oregon Has the Numbers to Back It Up.

We ranked all 50 US states using the National UFO Reporting Center (NUFORC), a national database that logs civilian UFO reports.

NUFORC flags individual reports as "Possible Abduction" or "Missing Time". We call these abduction-flagged reports, filed by people who believed they experienced direct contact or unexplained missing time.

Of 18,561 US reports filed between 2020 and 2024, 609 were abduction-flagged. Spread across 49 states. South Dakota: zero. Louisiana: one.

Key Findings

  • Vermont leads the US at 10.89 abduction-flagged reports per million residents. That is nearly double Oregon in second place.
  • 609 of 18,561 US reports filed between 2020 and 2024 were abduction-flagged. Spread across 49 states. South Dakota had zero. Louisiana had one.
  • Oregon leads on raw abduction-flagged reports with 28 but ranks second because Vermont's smaller population produces a higher per-person rate.
  • Three of the top six states are in the Pacific Northwest: Oregon (2nd), Washington (4th), and Alaska (6th). The observational conditions there are structurally different.
  • California has the most raw abduction-flagged reports of any state: 61. It ranks 32nd. 39 million residents absorb everything.

Cambridge Leads Britain. London Finishes Last.

We ranked 25 UK cities using UFO Identified, which collects sighting reports through Freedom of Information (FOI) requests to police forces across England, Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland, plus social media monitoring.

2020 to 2023 figures are confirmed. 2024 is estimated based on the previous four-year average.

Cambridge: 153 sightings per million. London: 11. The gap is the story.

Key Findings

  • Cambridge leads Britain at 153 sightings per million. That is 14 times higher than London, which finishes last of all 25 UK cities.
  • The top four UK cities are separated by just 12 sightings per million. Cambridge, Brighton, Middlesbrough, and Manchester are effectively in a statistical tie.
  • Manchester has the most raw sightings of any UK city: 78. It still ranks 4th. Over half a million residents dilute its lead.
  • London finishing last is a mathematical certainty. Nearly 9 million residents absorb any volume of sightings. The same dynamic plays out in Toronto and California.
